About

Calm, reliable data platforms.

CatInCloud Labs is a solo cloud and data engineering practice focused on AWS, Snowflake, and dbt pipelines that are calm to run in production.

How I think about the work

I care about systems that actually work in production: clear architecture, predictable behavior, and a level of complexity that matches the team operating it. CatInCloud Labs is run by me, Dave Anaya, a cloud and data engineer who tries to make platforms understandable, documented, and practical to live with.

I'm not trying to chase every new tool. I'd rather help you get more value out of what you already have, add structure where it's missing, and leave behind patterns your team feels comfortable owning.

Private by default, built to be lived with

A lot of my work starts with foundations: private networking, least-privilege IAM, KMS-encrypted storage, and observability that makes it clear when something is off. The aim is a stack you can rely on in day-to-day operations, not just during a demo.

From there, it's about steady, deliberate improvements, with a focus on well-scoped changes that fit your constraints and make life easier, rather than create technical debt.

The stack I focus on

I focus on AWS + Snowflake + dbt pipelines, often orchestrated with AWS MWAA (Airflow). That usually means: S3-based ingestion, Snowflake as the warehouse, dbt for transformations and tests, and Airflow for scheduling and coordination.

Those are the tools I know best, so I lean on patterns I've tested in my own reference stack and adapt them to your environment, team, and roadmap.

Want to talk through your stack?

If you're working with or thinking of adopting some version of this stack, and want things to feel calmer and more predictable, a short discovery call is a good place to start. We can walk through what you have today and see whether there's a useful project to tackle together.
