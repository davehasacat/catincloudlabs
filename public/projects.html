Enterprise Stock Market ELT (reference implementation)

A production-grade pipeline built on AWS, MWAA, Snowflake, and dbt, designed to ingest and transform stock and options data in a private VPC. It uses KMS-encrypted S3 buckets, VPC endpoints, and incremental dbt models to keep data flowing reliably from raw JSON to analytics-ready marts.

This is the same architecture pattern I adapt for client environments that want calm, well-understood data platforms.

At a glance

  • Stack: AWS (VPC, S3, MWAA), Snowflake, dbt
  • Pattern: private networking, KMS encryption, incremental models
  • Built for: calm, production-like behavior

Architecture and security posture

The high-level architecture and security/observability diagrams below show how the pieces fit together: MWAA in a private VPC, KMS-encrypted S3 buckets, Snowflake, and dbt all wired through VPC endpoints and least-privilege access. So what does this data tell us?

By comparing tickers to market benchmarks (SPY, QQQ) and a retail outlier (GME) the dashboard tells the full story of the market structure: Indices are for hedging, Meme stocks are for gambling, and NVDA has become a market of its own.

1. Flow proves the "Role" of the ticker

  • QQQ is used for protection. Look at the Options Chain table for QQQ. The top active contracts are frequently Puts (betting on downside). Institutions use these liquid indices to hedge their portfolios against tech selloffs.
  • GME is pure speculation. Switch the selector to GME. The top contracts are almost exclusively Calls. There is virtually no hedging visible here; the flow is entirely focused on speculative upside bursts.

2. NVDA rivals the S&P 500

  • The "New Index" behavior. Historically, SPY volumes dwarf single stocks. But in this window, NVDA's top contracts often traded higher volume than SPY's. This signals that for many traders, NVDA has effectively become the market proxy. NVDA is the center of gravity for liquidity and attention.

3. Volatility Regimes

  • Benchmarks anchor the room. SPY and QQQ maintain a steady, lower volatility profile (typically 10-15% annualized). They act as the "control group" against which the idiosyncratic risk of single names like GME (which lives in its own disconnected volatility reality) is measured.

Why this matters for Data Engineering

  • Handling scale and skew. This pipeline handles tickers with massively different profiles. It ingests data from the 1M+ daily contracts of NVDA/SPY to the bursty, erratic flow of GME, without manual intervention. It proves the ELT architecture can scale across the full "Market Weather" spectrum, from calm institutional flows to retail storms.

Private MWAA Environment

A hardened Airflow deployment running on AWS MWAA inside a fully private VPC. MWAA connects to ECR, CloudWatch Logs, KMS, Secrets Manager, and S3 through VPC endpoints. This ensures orchestration, logging, and image pulls all happen without public internet access.

The same approach can be used to keep your schedulers and control plane traffic private while still integrating cleanly with AWS services and your data warehouse.

In progress: Dashboards & apps on top

A browser-based analytics layer built with tools like Plotly Dash on top of Snowflake marts. The goal is to provide straightforward views into pipeline health and market data without adding another brittle surface area to maintain.

As the core platform stabilizes, these kinds of dashboards and lightweight apps become much easier to add safely.

Future: Machine learning on top of calm pipelines

The same stock + options pipeline that powers these charts is already keeping months of joined history in clean Snowflake marts. When it's the right time, that foundation can support light-weight machine learning without changing the core stack or adding a lot of moving parts.

  • Detect unusual options flow or volatility regimes for a given ticker, based on recent behavior.
  • Generate “what changed this week and why it matters” summaries directly from the daily features snapshot.
  • Build simple, well-behaved models to forecast volume, volatility, or regimes using only the data the pipeline already maintains.

Status: Not actively building yet. The focus today is on keeping the pipeline calm, explainable, and easy to operate, with the option to layer in ML later where it clearly helps decision making.
