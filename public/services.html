I don't sell open-ended consulting hours. I sell specific outcomes for teams running AWS + Snowflake + dbt stacks.

The Approach

Stabilization first, evolution second

Most teams are drowning in technical debt because they tried to build features before they built a foundation. My engagements are designed to fix the foundation first.

Whether we are auditing an existing mess or building a new capability, the goal is always the same: a system that is predictable, observable, and documented.

Sprint

The Platform Audit

A time-boxed review of your current stack to identify security risks, architectural bottlenecks, and reliability gaps.

  • Infrastructure Review: AWS networking, IAM roles, and encryption posture.
  • Data Logic Review: dbt model structure, test coverage, and DAG dependencies.
  • Deliverable: A prioritized roadmap outlining exactly what to fix and why.

Project

The Green-Field Build

For teams launching a new data capability. I architect and build the pipeline from source to dashboard, doing it right the first time.

  • Architecture: Private VPC design with MWAA and Snowflake PrivateLink.
  • Implementation: Ingestion scripts, dbt modeling (Bronze/Silver/Gold), and Airflow DAGs.
  • Handoff: Full documentation and a training session for your engineering team.

Retainer

Architectural Retainer

Ongoing senior guidance to keep your platform stable as your team ships new features.

  • Code Review: I review complex PRs to ensure no bad patterns slip into production.
  • Incident Response: Third-tier support for complex pipeline failures.
  • Advisory: Monthly syncs to plan roadmap and validate architectural decisions.

Process

How we work together

  1. Discovery. A 30-minute call to see if your stack (AWS/Snowflake/dbt) matches my expertise.
  2. Proposal. I send a clear SOW (Statement of Work) with fixed deliverables and timelines.
  3. Execution. I work in your GitHub repo and your Slack. You see progress daily.
  4. Handoff. I don't keep secrets. You get the code, the docs, and the knowledge.

Is this a fit?

We are a fit if:

  • You value correctness over speed.
  • You want stack decisions based on need, not dogma. My reference implementation is AWS/Snowflake, but I architect whatever the problem demands.
  • You need someone who can speak to both the CTO about budget and the Engineers about DAGs.
