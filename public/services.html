Services

Help for real-world AWS + Snowflake + dbt stacks.

I work with teams who already have something running and need it to behave like a calm, predictable system- not a fragile science project.

How I help

Stabilizing and evolving your data platform

I help teams build and stabilize AWS + Snowflake + dbt data platforms so they behave like real production systems. That usually means tightening up the architecture, getting pipelines under control, and making it much clearer who owns what.

A lot of work starts with a focused audit and hardening sprint: mapping how things actually work today, finding the brittle spots, and laying out a short, prioritized plan for fixes. From there, I can help implement the changes- whether that's cleaning up MWAA/Airflow, restructuring dbt models, improving observability, or getting data into Snowflake in a more deliberate way.

The goal is simple: give you a stack that's calmer to run and easier to evolve, without asking your team to adopt a brand-new tool every week or rebuild everything from scratch.

Sprint

Audit & hardening sprint

Short, time-boxed engagement to map your current stack, surface risks, and deliver a prioritized plan to stabilize it.

  • Architecture and data flow review
  • MWAA/Airflow and dbt project assessment
  • Risk and “brittle spots” identified
  • Clear list of “fix these first” actions

This is often the best place to start if things feel noisy, fragile, or hard to change.

Build

End-to-end pipeline builds

Design and implementation of AWS + Snowflake + dbt pipelines from ingestion through to analytics-ready models.

  • Ingestion into S3 and Snowflake
  • dbt models from RAW to marts
  • MWAA/Airflow orchestration and scheduling
  • Logging, alerting, and basic runbooks

The focus is on maintainable patterns your team can own, not a black-box implementation.

Support

Ongoing support & advisory

Lightweight, ongoing help to keep things stable and moving in the right direction once the basics are in place.

  • Regular check-ins and reviews
  • Small improvements and refactors
  • Second opinions on design decisions
  • Support for new features and use cases

Think of this as having a part-time cloud/data engineer who already understands your stack.

Working together

What a typical engagement looks like

  1. Discovery. Short call to understand your stack, pain points, and constraints.
  2. Audit / deep dive. Review of architecture, pipelines, dbt models, and observability.
  3. Plan. A clear, prioritized list of changes and a realistic path to get there.
  4. Implementation. Focused work on the pieces that will reduce the most pain and risk.
  5. Hand-off. Documentation and knowledge transfer so your team isn't dependent on me long-term.

Engagements are typically structured as a fixed-scope sprint or a short series of sprints, rather than open-ended hourly work.
