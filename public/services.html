How I help

Stabilizing and evolving your data platform

I help teams build and stabilize AWS + Snowflake + dbt data platforms so they behave like real production systems: predictable, observable, and easier to support.

Many engagements start with a focused audit & hardening sprint: understanding how things actually work today, identifying potential gaps, and agreeing on a short, prioritized list of improvements. From there, I can help implement the changes, whether that means shoring up Airflow, restructuring dbt models, tightening IAM and networking, or improving observability.

The goal is straightforward: a stack that does what it's supposed to do with fewer surprises, and that your team feels comfortable owning.

Sprint

Audit & Hardening Sprint

A short, time-boxed review of your current stack to understand what's running today and where the biggest risks are.

  • Review of current AWS / Snowflake / dbt / Airflow setup
  • Check for gaps in security, reliability, and cost
  • Clear summary of findings and trade-offs
  • Short, prioritized list of recommended changes

Good starting point if: you want an honest view of where things stand before committing to larger changes.

Build

End-to-End Pipeline Build

Design and implementation of AWS + Snowflake + dbt pipelines so data moves reliably from source to analytics-ready tables.

  • Architecture design for your AWS / warehouse stack
  • Ingestion, storage, and transformation patterns (dbt)
  • MWAA/Airflow orchestration and scheduling
  • Alerting, runbooks, and operating docs your team can follow

Good starting point if: you want a solid foundation your team can run day-to-day, with documentation they can actually use.

Support

Ongoing Ops & Enhancements

Lightweight ongoing help to keep your platform stable and steadily improving without hiring a full team.

  • Monitoring, alerting, and small reliability improvements
  • New sources, models, and data quality checks over time
  • Support for releases, schema changes, and refactors
  • Regular reviews so nothing quietly drifts off course

Good starting point if: you'd like steady help keeping things healthy while your team focuses on product and stakeholders.

Working together

What working together looks like

  1. Discovery call. Short conversation to understand your stack, pain points, and constraints, and to confirm there's a useful project to tackle.
  2. Plan & proposal. A clear, written outline of scope, approach, timeline, and pricing. The focus is on the pieces that will make the biggest difference.
  3. Implementation & handoff. Work delivered in small, reviewable chunks with code, diagrams, and operating docs your team owns, plus time for questions and knowledge transfer.

Most engagements are structured as a fixed-scope sprint or a short series of sprints, rather than open-ended hourly work.

Is CatInCloud Labs a fit for you?

CatInCloud Labs is a good fit for teams that want reliable data systems they can explain, trust, and build on.

Best fit if you:

  • Care about security, correctness, and maintainability as much as speed
  • Are using (or planning to use) AWS, Snowflake, dbt, and Airflow
  • Want one person who can cover architecture, data engineering, and DevOps instead of several separate vendors
  • Appreciate clear conversations about trade-offs, not just automatic agreement
