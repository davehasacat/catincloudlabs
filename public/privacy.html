Privacy Policy

Review the CatInCloud Labs privacy policy, including what data we collect through this site and how it is used.

Last updated: November 2025

What we collect

The only personal information we see is what you voluntarily share with us - typically your name, email address, and a short description of your project or question. You might send this either through our contact form or by emailing dave@catincloudlabs.com.

Contact form: Our contact form is powered by Formspree, a third-party service that securely processes and forwards your message to us by email. They may temporarily store form submissions on our behalf so we can receive and respond to them.

Server/CDN logs: Like most sites, our hosting and CDN may capture basic request metadata for security and operations, such as IP address, user agent, requested URL, and timestamps. We do not use this data for advertising or profiling.

How we use it

We use the information you provide solely to respond to your inquiry, understand whether and how we can help, and deliver any services you ask us to provide.

What we don't do

  • No advertising trackers or analytics cookies on this site.
  • No selling or renting your data. Ever.
  • No unnecessary data collection.

Retention

Inquiry emails and contact form submissions are retained only as long as reasonably needed to serve you and keep basic records - generally no longer than 12 months unless required for legal, accounting, or contractual reasons. Security and operational logs may be kept briefly to support troubleshooting and abuse prevention, then are rotated or deleted.

Your rights

You can request access, correction, or deletion of your information by emailing dave@catincloudlabs.com. We may verify requests via your email to prevent unauthorized changes. This policy applies to visitors of catincloudlabs.com and people who contact us by email or through the site's contact form.

Security

We operate with a security-first mindset (least privilege, private networking, encryption). For vulnerability reports, see our security.txt and our human-readable Security page.

Children's privacy

This site isn't directed to children under 13, and we don't knowingly collect their data. If you believe a child has provided us with personal information, please contact us so we can delete it.

Changes to this policy

We may update this page from time to time to reflect how the site actually operates. When we do, we'll revise the “Last updated” date above.
